# Research questions

## Questions the core build should answer first

### 1. Did statewide rent caps reduce occupied-rent growth?

Core outcome:
- ACS state median gross rent

Why it is answerable:
- clean state-year outcome,
- direct public API access,
- aligns well with statewide treatment coding.

### 2. Did statewide rent caps reduce renter cost burden?

Core outcome:
- ACS rent burden share at 30%+ of income

Why it matters:
- rent caps can lower incumbent housing cost growth even if asking rents remain high.

### 3. Did statewide rent caps increase residential stability?

Core outcomes:
- share in same house one year ago,
- share moved last year,
- share moved from a different state.

Why it matters:
- displacement and mobility are a central mechanism.

### 4. Did statewide rent caps reduce housing supply or shift the composition of supply?

Core outcomes:
- total permitted units,
- 5+ unit permits,
- multifamily share of permits.

Why it is strong:
- BPS is stable, official, and already staged in this project.

### 5. Did statewide rent caps change housing price dynamics?

Core outcome:
- FHFA HPI, state purchase-only index.

Why it matters:
- capitalization can show whether policy changes are reflected in owner-side values.

### 6. Did statewide rent caps change labor-market outcomes?

Core outcomes:
- QCEW employment,
- QCEW average weekly wage.

Why it matters:
- housing affordability changes can affect labor-market retention and regional competitiveness.

---

## Secondary questions for the core build

### 7. Were effects larger in states with higher baseline rent burden?

Operationalization:
- interact treatment with pre-policy ACS burden levels.

### 8. Were effects larger where supply was already constrained?

Operationalization:
- interact treatment with pre-policy permit rates or multifamily share.

### 9. Did impacts differ for rent levels versus burden versus mobility?

This helps separate:
- incumbent price effects,
- affordability effects,
- sorting / displacement effects.

### 10. Were California and Oregon similar enough to pool?

The build should estimate:
- separate models,
- stacked event studies,
- pooled treatment with state-specific event interactions.

## Questions reserved for phase 2 extensions

### 11. Did asking rents diverge from occupied rents?

Potential sources:
- Zillow / Apartment List
- local registries

### 12. Did formal evictions fall or change composition?

Potential source:
- Eviction Lab
- local eviction categories where available

### 13. Did housing quality or code violations change?

Potential source:
- NYC HPD violations
- AHS or local inspection data

### 14. Did legal rent ceilings diverge from market trends in legacy local systems?

Potential source:
- Berkeley registry
- St. Paul local records

---

## Questions this project should not claim to answer cleanly in phase 1

- exact covered-unit treatment status in local legacy systems,
- informal side payments or tenant screening intensity,
- landlord profitability or maintenance spending at scale,
- full general-equilibrium welfare,
- a single universal effect of “rent control” everywhere.
