# Local extensions

These are real opportunities, but they are not phase 1.

## Extension A: Berkeley

Best for:
- legal ceilings,
- registry-style mechanism work,
- divergence between legal and market rents.

Why deferred:
- bespoke local logic,
- not necessary for first statewide build.

## Extension B: St. Paul

Best for:
- modern local ordinance case study,
- amendment analysis.

Why deferred:
- local institutional detail matters a lot,
- easier once the generic pipeline exists.

## Extension C: NYC

Best for:
- maintenance / quality mechanisms,
- building-level linkage work.

Why deferred:
- the public list identifies buildings with at least one stabilized unit, not exact stabilized apartments,
- treatment assignment is more complex.

## Extension D: Evictions

Best for:
- formal displacement channel.

Why deferred:
- not needed to answer the core first-pass questions.

## Rule for choosing an extension

Pick exactly one local extension once the core statewide pipeline is stable.
